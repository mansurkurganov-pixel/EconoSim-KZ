Why EconoSim KZ?

The Problem

Economics is taught passively—no hands-on experience, no real decision-making. Students graduate without understanding how policy choices create consequences.

Our Solution

EconoSim KZ is the first economic simulator built on real Kazakhstan data. Players become economic advisors with full control over monetary, fiscal, structural, and social policies. Every decision creates ripple effects.

What Makes It Unique

✓ Built on Kazakhstan's real economic statistics (2015-2024)
✓ 8 interconnected KPIs reflecting real policy relationships
✓ Random external shocks (oil crashes, recessions, droughts)
✓ Immediate feedback on policy effectiveness
✓ No external libraries—pure web technology

🎯 Practical Learning

Test economic theories through gameplay. Cause-and-effect relationships become intuitive.

🇰🇿 Real Kazakhstan Data

Simulation powered by actual statistics, inflation rates, and structural conditions of Kazakhstan.

⚡ Instant Feedback

See how inflation, unemployment, and growth respond to your policies in real-time.

How the Game Works

1

Choose Your Starting Point

Select your starting year (2015-2019) and difficulty mode. Set your initial economic policy stance.

2

Control 5 Policy Systems

Manage monetary policy (rates, exchange regime), fiscal policy (taxes, spending), structural initiatives, external relations, and social programs.

3

React to Economic Shocks

Navigate random events: oil price crashes, global recessions, droughts, or investment surges. Test your crisis management skills.

4

Get Your Final Grade

After 40 quarters (10 years), receive an A-F grade based on economic targets. Compare your performance to real Kazakhstan history.

Technology Stack

Frontend Architecture

  • HTML5 - Semantic markup & Canvas API
  • CSS3 - Dark theme with gradients & animations
  • Vanilla JavaScript - No frameworks, pure ES6+
  • Canvas Graphics - Regional maps & trend charts
  • Google Fonts - Inter typeface

Game Engine & Data

  • Economic Model - Real policy relationships
  • Static JSON Data - Kazakhstan historical stats
  • Dynamic Simulation - Quarter-to-quarter calculations
  • Event System - Random shock generation
  • State Management - Game state persistence
